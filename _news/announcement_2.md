Our paper **TIGER: Bridging the Multimodal Reasoning-Access Gap via Modality Counterfactuals** has been accepted as a **poster presentation** at the **ICML 2026 FoGen Workshop**!

---

#### About TIGER

TIGER (Multimodal Reasoning-Access Gap via Modality Counterfactuals) addresses the gap between multimodal reasoning capabilities and access to multimodal information. The paper proposes a novel approach using **modality counterfactuals** to enhance reasoning in vision-language models.

#### Key Contributions

<ul>
    <li>Novel counterfactual learning approach for multimodal reasoning</li>
    <li>Bridging the gap between reasoning capabilities and access</li>
    <li>Evaluation on standard vision-language benchmarks</li>
</ul>

This work is a collaboration with **Gregory Kang Ruey Lau** and my advisor **Prof. Bryan Kian Hsiang Low** from the National University of Singapore.
